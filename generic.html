INSTRUCTOR

An Instructor teaches and guides students or professionals in a specific subject area. They develop educational content, deliver lectures or training sessions, and assess learners' progress. Instructors can work in academic institutions, corporate training departments, or offer their services online through platforms.

SKILLS

Data Analyst

A Data Analyst collects, processes, and performs statistical analyses on large datasets to identify trends, patterns, and insights. They use various tools such as SQL, Excel, and data visualization software like Tableau or Power BI to present findings. Their goal is to help organizations make data-driven decisions.

Data Scientist

A Data Scientist builds on the role of a Data Analyst by using advanced statistical methods, machine learning, and predictive modeling to analyze complex data sets. They often use programming languages like Python and R, along with tools such as TensorFlow and Hadoop. Data Scientists design and implement algorithms to solve business problems and create data-driven products.

Web Developer

A Web Developer designs, builds, and maintains websites and web applications. They work with languages like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks like React or Angular for front-end development, and PHP, Python, Ruby, or Node.js for back-end development. Their role is to ensure websites are functional, user-friendly, and visually appealing.

Freelancer

A Freelancer is a self-employed professional who offers services to clients on a project or contract basis. Freelancers work in various fields, such as writing, graphic design, programming, consulting, and more. They manage their own business operations, including marketing, client relations, and financial management.
